Overview

A Mind Ahead of Its Century

Born in 1856 in Smiljan, in the Austrian Empire, Nikola Tesla would go on to hold hundreds of patents across a dozen countries, laying the groundwork for the alternating-current systems that power the modern world.

After studying engineering in Graz and Prague, Tesla emigrated to the United States in 1884 with little more than a letter of introduction and a head full of ideas that would soon reshape how humanity generated, transmitted, and used electricity.

His rivalry with Thomas Edison over AC versus DC power, his radical wireless-energy experiments at Wardenclyffe, and his lifelong pursuit of ideas the world wasn't yet ready for have made him one of history's most enduring symbols of visionary genius.

Did You Know

Interesting Facts

01

Tesla could allegedly speak eight languages fluently.

He was fluent in Serbian, English, Czech, German, French, Hungarian, Italian, and Latin, and claimed an eidetic (photographic) memory that let him visualize entire inventions in his mind before ever building a prototype.

02

He envisioned global wireless communication decades early.

His Wardenclyffe Tower project aimed to transmit information — and eventually free electrical power — wirelessly across the globe, a concept that wouldn't be fully realized until Wi-Fi and cellular networks over a century later.

03

Tesla never married, citing his work as his true devotion.

He often said celibacy helped him focus entirely on his inventions, and was known for forming close, admiring friendships instead — including a well-documented fondness for the pigeons he fed daily in New York parks.

04

He died with 300+ patents but in financial hardship.

Despite his enormous scientific output, Tesla's later-life projects struggled to find funding, and he died in 1943 in a modest New York hotel room — his full recognition largely arriving decades after his death.
